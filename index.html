Horizontals I

Choy Weng Yang

Horizontals I demonstrates how the artist Choy Weng Yang uses colours to express emotions. His expressive and uneven treatment of paint here is indicative of his emotive and intuitive, rather than scientific, approach to colour.

Black and White

Anthony Poon

The precise geometric order and contrasting colours in this work create a Hermann grid, an optical illusion where one sees illusory grey spots at the intersections of lines. Its network of silver lines was based not only on visual appeal but on theories of colour.

Skyline of New York

Ahmad Sadali

Ahmad Sadali believed that modern art forms best reflected modern realities. Here, he produces an abstract view of New York City's waterfront using thin lines that intersect and cut across its buildings. These lines create a shattered picture of the city while simultaneously giving it a sense of unity.
